Determinants of Travel Destination Choice Intention Among Vietnamese Tourists: The Case of Hoi An World Cultural Heritage Site

An individual graduation project examining how destination image, destination features, tourism infrastructure, perceived behavioral control, economic concerns, and social influence shape Vietnamese tourists’ intention to choose Hoi An.

Project Overview

This graduation project investigates the determinants of travel destination choice intention among Vietnamese tourists, using Hoi An World Cultural Heritage Site as the research context. The study was designed to identify which internal and external factors most strongly influence tourists’ intention to choose Hoi An in their travel itinerary.

Research Objectives

Main Objectives

  • Identify the factors influencing Vietnamese tourists’ intention to choose Hoi An.
  • Clarify the relative roles of destination image, destination features, infrastructure, perceived behavioral control, economic concerns, and social influence.
  • Measure which factors have the strongest effect on destination choice intention.
  • Discuss practical recommendations for Hoi An tourism marketing and destination management.

Why This Project Matters

  • Focuses specifically on Vietnamese domestic tourists rather than international visitors.
  • Provides evidence for tourism strategy in a heritage destination context.
  • Connects behavioral theory with practical destination marketing decisions.
  • Demonstrates strong quantitative research and analytical skills.

Research Model

The model tested six factors affecting travel destination choice intention:

Destination Image Destination Features Tourism Infrastructure Perceived Behavioral Control Economic Concerns Social Influence Travel Destination Choice Intention

Methodology

1. Research Design

  • Applied a quantitative, cross-sectional research design.
  • Used a structured questionnaire as the main data collection instrument.
  • Focused on Vietnamese tourists who had visited or intended to choose Hoi An.
Quantitative Cross-sectional Tourism Research

2. Measurement

  • Used 34 observed variables adapted from previous studies.
  • Measured responses with a 5-point Likert scale.
  • Included constructs related to destination perception, economics, infrastructure, and social influence.
Questionnaire Likert Scale Scale Validation

3. Data Collection

  • Collected data from September to November 2025.
  • Used both online and in-person survey distribution.
  • Obtained 433 valid responses after screening.
Field Survey Online Survey 433 Samples

4. Data Analysis

  • Analyzed the model using SmartPLS 4 and PLS-SEM.
  • Tested reliability, convergent validity, discriminant validity, and structural relationships.
  • Evaluated outer loadings, Cronbach’s alpha, AVE, HTMT, VIF, R², and f².
SmartPLS PLS-SEM Hypothesis Testing

Sample Highlights

Respondent Profile

  • 433 valid responses were used for analysis.
  • The sample was dominated by younger Vietnamese tourists.
  • The 18–24 age group represented the largest share.
  • Most respondents traveled 1–2 times per year and had moderate trip budgets.

Interpretation

  • The sample reflects a young domestic tourist segment.
  • This group is highly connected to social media and sensitive to value-for-money.
  • It is especially relevant for destination marketing aimed at Vietnamese travelers.

Key Findings

Social Influence

  • Had the strongest positive effect on destination choice intention.
  • Shows the importance of friends, family, online communities, and social media.
  • Suggests that destination choice is strongly shaped by shared opinions and digital visibility.
Social Media eWOM Decision Influence

Perceived Behavioral Control

  • Was the second strongest predictor of intention.
  • Indicates the importance of time, money, information, and travel convenience.
  • Suggests that tourists are more likely to choose Hoi An when they feel capable of managing the trip.
Travel Confidence Convenience Decision Readiness

Destination Features & Infrastructure

  • Both had positive and significant effects on destination choice intention.
  • Heritage, scenery, cultural experiences, and infrastructure quality all mattered.
  • Shows that both core attraction and service convenience shape destination preference.
Heritage Value Experience Infrastructure

Destination Image & Economic Concerns

  • Both positively influenced destination choice intention.
  • Destination image helped strengthen desire and emotional connection.
  • Economic concerns mattered, but played a smaller role than social influence and control perception.
Image Value for Money Tourist Perception

Model Strength

Measurement Model

  • Outer loadings generally met the accepted threshold.
  • Cronbach’s alpha and composite reliability were satisfactory.
  • AVE values exceeded the minimum threshold for convergent validity.

Structural Model

  • All six hypotheses were supported.
  • R² reached 0.828, indicating a very strong explanatory level.
  • The model showed strong overall fit for explaining destination choice intention.

Practical Implications

  • Hoi An should strengthen social media communication and user-generated content strategies.
  • Tourism managers should improve clarity, convenience, and trip-planning support for visitors.
  • Destination marketing should highlight cultural heritage, local experiences, and distinct identity.
  • Infrastructure and service quality should support both access and tourist comfort.
  • Pricing strategies should focus on value-for-money rather than only low-cost offers.
