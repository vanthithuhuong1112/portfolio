Project Overview

“Labor Market Report 2024” was a hybrid workshop organized for students in the collaboration program between HELP University and the University of Finance and Marketing. The event aimed to provide an overview of the Vietnamese labor market, recruitment trends, and practical career orientation for students in fields such as Marketing, International Business, Finance, and Accounting.

Project Snapshot

Key Information

  • Event: Labor Market Report 2024
  • Date: July 17, 2024
  • Time: 2:00 PM – 4:00 PM
  • Venue: UFM Main Campus — B.002, C.002, C.003
  • Format: Hybrid (online & offline)

Event Objectives

  • Provide updated labor market insights for students.
  • Share recruitment trends and skills needed in 2024.
  • Create a practical Q&A space with an HR industry speaker.
  • Support students’ career orientation and job preparation.

My Role — Administration Finance

In this project, I was responsible for the Administration Finance function, supporting the event through cost control and logistical coordination.

  • Calculated costs for event activities and supported budget control.
  • Prepared a gift for the speaker, and candies for the audience while waiting and attending the event.
  • Found a printing vendor for the standee.
  • Calculated the right for sponsors, speaker, and organization.
  • Supported the planning and arrangement for the event positioning.

How the Event Was Executed

1. Planning & Preparation

  • Team members were assigned into event management, program coordination, venue, hospitality, and finance roles.
  • The team prepared the budget, registration flow, media promotion, and room setup in advance.
  • Poster, standee, registration form, and feedback survey were prepared before the event day.
Planning Budgeting Coordination

2. Hybrid Setup

  • The main room could not accommodate all participants, so the team arranged extra rooms.
  • Microsoft Teams was used to connect the main session to hybrid rooms.
  • This allowed the organizers to serve more students despite venue limitations.
Hybrid Event Teams Problem Solving

3. On-site Operations

  • Guests were checked in and seated before the event started.
  • The program included introduction, main presentation, Q&A, and feedback/gift distribution.
  • The team coordinated attendees across both the main and sub-rooms.
Execution Check-in Operations

4. Post-event Evaluation

  • The team collected feedback and reviewed participant satisfaction.
  • Insights were discussed to evaluate venue suitability, content quality, and event experience.
  • Lessons learned were documented for future hybrid events.
Evaluation Feedback Improvement

Key Outcomes

Event Results

  • The event attracted strong student interest and higher-than-expected attendance.
  • The hybrid model helped the team accommodate more participants than the main room capacity.
  • The workshop successfully delivered labor market insights and career-related knowledge.

What I Contributed

  • Supported cost efficiency through finance-related planning.
  • Handled practical logistics such as speaker gift and standee vendor sourcing.
  • Contributed to smooth event preparation through detail-oriented administrative support.

Lessons Learned

  • Venue capacity must be considered more carefully before opening registration widely.
  • Hybrid events require stronger technical backup for internet and audio stability.
  • Clear participant communication and visible schedule information improve attendee experience.
  • Coordination across roles is critical when handling both offline and online audiences.

Skills Demonstrated
