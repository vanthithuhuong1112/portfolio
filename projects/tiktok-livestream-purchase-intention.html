Project Overview

This research project examines the factors that influence Generation Z’s purchase intention on the TikTok livestream platform. The study focuses on how social proof, pricing, platform usability, and information quality shape consumer behavior in a fast-growing social commerce environment.

Research Objectives

Main Goal

  • Identify the factors that significantly influence Gen Z purchase intention on TikTok livestream.
  • Understand how TikTok livestream functions as an effective e-commerce environment.
  • Explore how platform experience and promotional factors shape buying behavior.

Research Questions

  • Which factors most strongly affect Gen Z purchase intention on TikTok livestream?
  • How do influencer endorsement and customer reviews affect buying behavior?
  • Why does Gen Z choose TikTok livestream over other online shopping platforms?
  • How do price, usefulness, ease of use, and information quality shape purchase intention?

Research Model

The study examined six independent variables affecting purchase intention:

Influencer Endorsement Customer Reviews Perceived Ease of Use Perceived Usefulness Low-Price Appeal Perceived Information Quality Purchase Intention

Methodology

1. Research Design

  • Used a quantitative research approach.
  • Applied survey-based data collection to test research hypotheses.
  • Focused on Gen Z users engaging with TikTok livestream shopping.
Quantitative Survey Consumer Behavior

2. Measurement

  • Used a 23-item questionnaire adapted from previous studies.
  • Measured responses using a 5-point Likert scale.
  • Included constructs for reviews, influencers, usability, usefulness, price, information quality, and purchase intention.
Questionnaire Likert Scale Constructs

3. Data Collection

  • Distributed the survey online through platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, and Messenger.
  • Collected responses from Vietnamese consumers.
  • Used valid responses for data screening and analysis.
Online Survey Vietnam Data Screening

4. Data Analysis

  • Analyzed demographic data and descriptive statistics.
  • Tested reliability, convergent validity, and discriminant validity.
  • Used SmartPLS / PLS-SEM to test the structural model and hypotheses.
SmartPLS PLS-SEM Hypothesis Testing

Sample Highlights

Respondent Profile

  • 306 usable responses were analyzed.
  • Most respondents belonged to the Gen Z age group.
  • Female respondents represented a larger share of the sample.
  • Many respondents had monthly income below 10 million VND.

Why This Matters

  • The sample fits the target audience of TikTok livestream shopping.
  • It helps explain how younger consumers respond to social commerce triggers.
  • It also shows how pricing and convenience influence digital purchase behavior.

Key Findings

Low-Price Appeal

  • Had the strongest positive impact on purchase intention.
  • Suggests that discounts, promotions, and attractive pricing strongly drive conversion.
  • Highlights the importance of value-for-money in livestream selling.
Price Strategy Promotions Conversion

Influencer Endorsement

  • Positively influenced purchase intention.
  • Shows the importance of trust, social proof, and influencer relatability.
  • Supports the role of creator-driven commerce on TikTok.
Influencers Trust Social Proof

Customer Reviews

  • Showed a small but negative effect on purchase intention.
  • Indicates that review quality and credibility can reduce trust when the content feels unconvincing.
  • Suggests the need for better review management in social commerce.
Reviews Reputation Trust Risk

Usefulness, Ease of Use & Information Quality

  • Perceived usefulness and information quality had positive effects on purchase intention.
  • Perceived ease of use also had a positive effect, though smaller than price and usefulness.
  • Suggests that clear information and frictionless user experience support livestream sales.
User Experience Information Quality Utility

Business Implications

  • Businesses should emphasize competitive pricing and promotional appeal when selling through livestream.
  • Influencer partnerships can increase trust and improve purchase intention.
  • Accurate, useful, and easy-to-understand product information should be prioritized.
  • Improving platform usability and reducing friction can support better conversion.
  • Negative reviews should be monitored carefully because they can weaken purchase intention.

What I Learned

  • Strengthened my understanding of TikTok-based consumer behavior and social commerce drivers.
  • Practiced academic research design, questionnaire construction, and variable-based analysis.
  • Built stronger analytical thinking around how pricing, content, and trust influence buying decisions.
  • Connected business research concepts with practical implications for TikTok Shop and livestream commerce.
